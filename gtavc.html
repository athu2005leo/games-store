Grand Theft Auto Vice City

GTA Vice City is an open world action-adventure game developed by Rockstar Games. Grand Theft Auto: Vice City is a classic action-adventure open-world video game originally released by Rockstar Games in 2002. Set in a fictional, neon-soaked 1980s Miami, players control mobster Tommy Vercetti as he builds a criminal empire following a botched drug deal.The game is famous for its cultural impact, combining crime-drama gameplay with a satirical take on 1980s pop culture. Key features that define the game include:Iconic Protagonist: Voiced by the late Ray Liotta, Tommy Vercetti is highly inspired by Tony Montana from the film Scarface.Building an Empire: Unlike previous titles in the franchise, players can purchase and manage various real estate properties and businesses—such as a taxi company or a nightclub—to generate ongoing cash.Legendary Soundtrack: The game features an immersive licensed soundtrack from the 1980s, broadcast on in-game radio stations playing pop, rock, new wave, and hip-hop.Atmosphere: Vice City boasts a tropical, sun-drenched aesthetic with beaches, swamps, fast cars, and neon-lit cityscapes.The game has since been ported to numerous platforms, including mobile devices and modern consoles as part of the Grand Theft Auto: The Trilogy – The Definitive Edition
