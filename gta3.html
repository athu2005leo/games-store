Grand Theft Auto III

GTA III is an open world action-adventure game developed by Rockstar Games. Grand Theft Auto III (released in 2001) is a groundbreaking action-adventure game developed by Rockstar North (formerly DMA Design) and published by Rockstar Games. It is widely celebrated as the title that revolutionized the 3D open-world genre. Wikipedia +2 Core Information Release Date: October 22, 2001 (initially on PlayStation 2). Rockstar Games +1 Setting: The fictional, neo-noir Liberty City (loosely based on New York City). It is divided into three distinct islands: Portland, Staunton Island, and Shoreside Vale. Wikipedia +3 Protagonist: Claude, a silent criminal seeking revenge after being betrayed by his girlfriend during a bank heist. GTA Wiki +1 Gameplay: The game features non-linear, open-ended gameplay where players can explore the city, commandeer vehicles, complete story-based missions, and engage in various sandbox-style activities. Facebook ·IGI Key Features Revolutionary 3D Engine: It was the first 3D game in the Grand Theft Auto series, transitioning the franchise away from its 2D, top-down roots. Wikipedia +1 Satirical Culture: The game is known for its darkly comic tone, satirical talk radio stations, and realistic (for the time) urban environment. Voice Acting: GTA III boasted stellar Hollywood voice talent, including Michael Rapaport (as Joey Leone) and Joe Pantoliano (as Luigi Goterelli). Legacy & Availability Grand Theft Auto III set the foundation for subsequent games in the series (such as Vice City and San Andreas) and completely shifted the gaming industry's focus toward sandbox exploration.
